Sum and Product of Roots in Quadratic Equations

Explanation of sum and product of roots in quadratic equations for my classmates
May 29, 2021 · colossal_pepe
Table of Contents
  • What will be explained
  • Explanation

What will be explained#

The 2 claims below will be explained:

If $a \neq 0$, in any equation $ax^2+bx+c=0$, let the roots of this equation be $\alpha$ and $\beta$.

Claim 1: $\alpha + \beta = {-b \over a}$

Claim 2: $\alpha \beta = {c \over a}$

Explanation#

Let $\alpha = {-p \over o}$ and $\beta = {-r \over q}$. Hence, we have: $$ (ox+p)(qx+r) = 0 \\[0.7em] \implies ox(qx+r) + p(qx+r) = 0 \\[0.7em] \implies oqx^2+orx+pqx+pr = 0 \\[0.7em] \implies oqx^2+(or+pq)x+pr = 0 \\[0.7em] $$ In here $a=oq,b=or+pq,c=pr$.

Claim 1: $$ \alpha + \beta = {-p \over o} + {-r \over q} \\[0.7em] = {-pq-ro \over oq} \\[0.7em] = {-(or+pq) \over oq} = {-b \over a} $$ Claim 2: $$ \alpha \beta = {-p \over o} \cdot {-r \over q} \\[0.7em] = {pr \over oq} = {c \over a} $$ Thank you for coming to my TEDTalk.
